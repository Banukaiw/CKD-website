Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is a global public health issue that affects roughly 10% of the world’s population, and one million people die from untreated kidney failure each year worldwide [1]. CKD is a very common and problematic disease in low and middle-income countries, including Sri Lanka. Chronic kidney disease of uncertain etiology (CKDU), a new and prevalent kind of CKD, is affected in several regions of Sri Lanka, especially in the North Central Province [2]. High concentrations of heavy metals, fluoride, cyanobacterial and algae toxins, and agrochemicals in water have been highlighted as risk factors in investigations conducted in Sri Lanka [3], [4].

CKD is a pathologic syndrome that progresses and is irreversible. Once the kidneys are damaged or near-complete kidney failure occurs, patients need to go through dialysis or a kidney transplant. These are very difficult tasks, both economically and resource-wise [5]. The disease can be easily cured by detecting it in its early stages. However, because early CKD does not have any apparent symptoms, patients do not recognize the illness. Early detection and treatment of CKD will prevent the disease from progressing to kidney failure. Therefore, the prediction and identification of CKD in its early stages are necessary.

Identification & classification

There is no proper system to identify chronic kidney patients in the Sri Lankan southern province hospitals. The patient’s blood and urine report records should be in a smart data entry system and real time database for this system. The web application based smart solution for chronic kidney patients’ identification and disease identification and forecasting system for proposed hospital in southern province.

Severity assessment

There are a number of initiatives underway to improve the identification and treatment of CKD in Sri Lanka. These include the development of a national CKD screening program and the establishment of specialized CKD clinics. However, these initiatives have been hampered by a lack of funding and resources.

Information sharing

Real time communication system to speed up information sharing between chronic kidney patients , Doctors and hospital nurses has been identified as priority need for effective chronic kidney disease control in the Sri Lankan southern province.

Proposed Problem

How to Address the rising chronic kidney disease in Sri Lanka using machine learning for accurate diagnosis and treatment?

Over the years, chronic kidney disease has emerged as a major public health problem in Sri Lanka. health of the human body. Improper diagnosis and treatment of the disease can eventually lead to end-stage renal disease and ultimately lead to the patient’s death. In the present research work, we aim to identify and predict kidney diseases by using machine learning algorithms.

Nowadays people suffer from various diseases, but they clearly can’t identify what are the diseases they have. Also, people treat various diseases without understanding their real diseases. By using this system, the chronic kidney disease symptoms and clearly identify the illnesses we can predict the kidney diseases and what is the type of illnesses and forecast the medicine and medicine requirements for patients.


Proposed Solution

First entering the kidney disease symptoms dataset and the kidney diseases dataset of patient’s using machine learning algorithm system will predict the chronic kidney diseases. And then the system will diagnosis the chronic kidney diseases.

After taking the blood and urine scanning data and the patients kidney disease data, we suppose to using machine learning algorithms, forecast the ayurvedic treatment for the chronic kidney disease patients.

Then by interring the hospital drugs details and equipment data to the system and system will predict and forecast the future drugs and medical equipment for the next years for the proposed hospital in southern province in Sri Lanka.

We will get the details of the patient’s nutritional details. And also get the details of their medicines and meal details they consume and body weight details. Then by using machine learning algorithms we will suggest a healthy diet plan to the kidney patients.
